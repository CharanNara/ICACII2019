Theoretical design and experimental study for urban data management using energy-saved IoT big data

M Jayanthi and K Pravallika

UCET Mahatma Gandhi University Nalgonda

ABSTRACT

This paper proposes an energy-saving IoT based framework (ESIF) for urban data management and smart city application using big data analytics. The proposed concept is also used to manage the energy consumption in urban planning and smart city applications. Previous researches states that the big data analysis is complex. Hence, our proposed study contains different kinds of sensors such as traffic congestion, weather, smart home, water and vehicular sensor to collect the data. The proposed framework is divided into three phases. The first phase is responsible for transferring and storing the big data in hadoop and the second phase is responsible to measure the energy consumption in both city devices and data center resources. The third phase deals with the data processing. In the data processing phase, decision making is important step, which is used to classify the events and produce the decisions. To do this, machine learning algorithms are used and generate the decisions effectively. The Hadoop is to provide offline service and the online services are performed with the help of spark. Therefore, the effectiveness of the proposed model is illustrated through a case of processing time and throughput. Ultimately, more scalable, flexible proposed concept is implemented and it provide better performance field.
