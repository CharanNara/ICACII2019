ASSESSMENT OF CROP ROTATION USING MULTI POLARIZED SENTINEL-1 TEMPORAL SAR DATA OVER PARTS OF KUMAUN REGION OF UTTARAKHAND

Aditya Allamraju and Hari Shanker Srivastava

CSSTEAP, Indian Institute of Remote Sensing (IIRS/ISRO)

ABSTRACT

Accurate information about crop rotation is essential for administrators, managers and various government departments for assessment, monitoring, and management of various resources for crop intensification. Radar remote sensing, due to its all-weather capability and assured uninterrupted data supply, can play an important role in the evaluation of crop rotation. Twentynine (29) Level-1 Ground Range Detected (GRD) Sentinel-1 C band scenes acquired in the Interferometric Wide (IW) swath mode during the period from 11th November 2017 to 30th November 2018 over Kumaun region, Uttarakhand were utilized to explain the feasibility of multi-temporal Synthetic Aperture Radar (SAR) data for crop rotation. Principal Component Analysis (PCA) was adopted to demarcate different crop rotation trends throughout the study area. The results show that ‘wheat to paddy’ is the highest practised crop rotation with 30.39% coverage, whereas ‘sugarcane to paddy’ is the lowest practised crop rotation with 2.60% coverage of the study area provided with an overall accuracy of 80% acquired from the ground-truth data from IIRS/ISRO. It is a benefit for the decision-makers, administrators and policymakers to take reliable decisions about a particular region regarding land management and natural resources.
