A Comparative Analysis on Rumor Microblogs Detection from Social Network Sites

Mohammed Mahmood Ali, Abdul Rahiman . and Syed Abdul Sattar

Rayalaseema University

ABSTRACT

Social media platforms emerged as a trending instrument for newsworthy events because it is fast, easy to access and relatively cheap. Subsequently, spreading of falsify information may lead to the emergence and spread of rumors that leads towards serious damage to government and private sector, adversely affect the business market and society. To overcome, there is an immense necessity to build models for detecting rumors as early as possible before they extensively spread viral in the society. Very less work was done on rumor detection. The earlier models which were developed till now detect predefined rumors to certain limits and for the newly emerging rumors there must be a strong model. These models which are previously defined for detecting new emerging rumors are not effective in predicting the rumors. This paper illustrates a brief literature review on rumor detection models that were proposed earlier for social networking sites. Further, the pros and consequences of each of the paper is thoroughly discussed and suggestions were given for improving the existing rumor detection models. Many of the rumor detection models utilizes Data mining techniques, SVM technique, Random Graph Theory models, RNN model, CRF classifier, Semantic graph structures, Propagation tree structures, propagation structures and others. Ultimately, it is suggested that Rumor models can be improvised by embedding of Predefined Logical Rumor rules and Semantic Web ontology instead of lying only on one specific technique.
