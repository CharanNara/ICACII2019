Moving Hand Segmentation from H.264 Compressed Sign Language Videos

Kaushik Mazumdar, Anjan Kumar Talukdar and Kandarpa Kumar Sarma

Gauhati University

ABSTRACT

Hand segmentation is the most critical step in sign language recognition. Many advanced video applications require manipulation of compressed domain video signals. In general, video signals are compressed due to the limitations of storage and transmission bandwidth of networks. Therefore, we proposed a system to segment hand gesture from a compressed domain video file. The only cues used for segmentation are the motion vectors (MVs). First, the MVs are pre-processed to get the motion information accurately that are related to the moving hand. The decision of a particular block belongs to an object or non-object is done with the help of Markov Random Field (MRF) based classifiers. The experimental result of this paper includes the testing of the proposed system for American Sign Language (ASL) and Indian Sign Language (ISL) under different environmental conditions of the background. The proposed system shows an average precision, recall and F-measure of around 94.19%, 62.14%, and 75.11% respectively.
