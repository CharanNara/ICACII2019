Analysing Students' Performance using Data Mining Technique

V K Anishaa Raj, Varshith Komirisetty,
Sathvika Poreddy and Sandeep Singh Rawat

ABSTRACT

The paper represents the data mining techniques used for analyzing students’ performance. In general, every educational institution contains an enormous amount of academic database containing student details. Thus it is time-consuming and inaccurate to segregate students on the basis of their marks obtained. On the other hand, an automated system performs the same accurately and in a minimal amount of time. It will help us in identifying promising students and by providing us a chance to pay heed and to refine those students who likely get low marks. Clustering Algorithms like K-Means clustering can help us group students’ with similar scores obtained in various subjects. Each of these clustered groups can be considered as objects with similar academic behavior. Each of these groups is now treated with a common course of action. Thus, this analysis helps the school management and teachers to arrange and conduct additional training classes for students with average or below-average performance in the respective subjects. We are basically trying to improve student’s acquirement and success more effectively in a way using educational data mining techniques. It can bring benefits to the school by increasing their pass percentage and thereby enhancing their academic performance. This system also helps the teachers to identify weak students in their class so that more attention can be given to them. Experimental outcomes show that the proposed procedure significantly outperforms the existing procedures by preventing any misuse of students’ personal data component sets.
